Fried Chicken Recipe

Description

I experimented with lots of recipes over the years to find the best crispy fried chicken. This is it! It's bits and pieces of hints I have heard over the years. It's more a matter of preparation and cooking method!

This is the only crispy fried chicken recipe you'll ever need. Why? It combines all the tried-and-true tricks to ensure the juiciest, most flavorful, crispiest chicken every time.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Take your cut-up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer.
  2. Put the flour in a large plastic bag. Season it with paprika, salt, and pepper to taste. (Paprika helps to brown the chicken.)
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk, then add a few at a time to the bag of flour. Seal and shake to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a tray and cover with a clean towel or wax paper. Let it sit until the flour forms a paste-like consistency â€” this step is crucial!
  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until very hot.
  6. Add as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown both sides in hot oil.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover. Let cook for 30 minutes. Then uncover, raise heat, and fry until crispy.
  8. Drain on paper towels. If cooking in batches, keep finished chicken warm in the oven while frying the rest.
